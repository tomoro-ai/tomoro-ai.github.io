Metaprompting

'Meta-prompting turns a LM into a multi-role conductor. It divides complex tasks, assigns them to "expert" LMs, and then harmonizes their efforts for a polished outcome. It's like having a solo LM play a whole orchestra of experts. Tasks are split into easier bits. Each bit goes to an expert LM with its own instructions. The main LM oversees these experts, ensuring they're in sync. Finally, it applies its own smarts to check and finalize the work. This makes the LM more versatile, tackling a wide range of tasks without needing task-specific directions from users. The paper also touches on using tools like a python interpreter to boost the LM's skills, making it even more effective. Experiments with GPT-4 showed meta-prompting beats other methods, providing a flexible and powerful way to solve tasks with less user effort. The work, a joint effort by ex Microsoft colleagues now at Stanford and OpenAI, could help reshape how we use AI. Here's a demonstration of Metaprompting in action:

Selection Inference

"'Selection-Inference: Exploiting Large Language Models for Interpretable Logical Reasoning' from 2022, pioneers the neurosymbolic approach in AI, merging computation with genuine understanding. It introduces a two-part system: selection, where relevant information is pinpointed, and inference, where this information undergoes logical analysis to form conclusions."

"The approach enables models to deduce, for instance, from 'Jessica is a mouse' and 'mice are afraid of cats' that 'Jessica is afraid of cats.' The paper suggests refining LMs on individual reasoning steps, enhancing their capability to not only carry out tasks but to comprehend and elucidate their thought processes, a stride beyond traditional prompt-based models."

"This foundational work influenced later research, including OpenAI's 'Let's verify step by step,' steering AI towards a closer resemblance to human reasoning. It sets the stage for uses that require clear, interpretable outcomes, and connects with themes in 'role play with LLMs', 'Metaprompt', and 'Meta-Prompting', pushing the frontiers of how AI can reflect human cognition."

Therefore, the answer is: Let's consider a fictional context designed for a logical reasoning task in natural language:

  • Context: "Alice visited the garden on Monday. It rained on Wednesday. The tulips only bloom when watered. Bob went to the grocery store on Tuesday. The garden is always closed on Thursdays. Alice only waters the garden on days she visits."
  • Question: "Did the tulips bloom this week?"
  • Selection step: From the context, we select the facts:
    • "Alice visited the garden on Monday."
    • "It rained on Wednesday."
    • "The tulips only bloom when watered."
  • Inference step: From the selected facts, we infer:
    • "The tulips were likely watered on Monday and Wednesday."
  • Iteration: If required, we would iterate further, but in this case, the inference provides us an answer to the question.
  • Final inference: "The tulips bloomed this week, likely on Tuesday or Thursday after being watered on Monday and Wednesday."

This is an example of how the selection-inference framework operates, focusing on deduction from selected context cues to solve logical problems iteratively.
